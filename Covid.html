Prevention from Corona Virus

stay home, stay safe

how to prevent

how to prevent virus

clean your place

Disinfect your home when someone is sick or if someone who is positive for COVID-19 has been in your home within the last 24 hours. Disinfecting kills any remaining germs on surfaces and reduces the spread of germs.

maintain distance

Social distancing is crucial for preventing the spread of contagious illnesses such as COVID-19(Corona Virus). It’s important for people of all ages to maintain a distance of about 6 feet as a preventative measure.

don't touch face

Experts recommend not to touch the face because the virus is transmitted through mucous membranes in the mouth, nose and eyes. One can wear a face shield as a reminder to not touch their face.

wash your hand

To prevent the spread of germs during the COVID-19 pandemic, one should wash their hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds or use a hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol to avoid the spread.

cover your mouth

COVID-19 spreads mainly from person to person through respiratory droplets. Respiratory droplets travel into the air when you cough, sneeze, talk, shout, or sing. To avoid the spread, use a napkin or a handkerchief.

wear a mask

Make wearing a mask a normal part of being around other people. Make sure it covers both your nose, mouth and chin. Wash it or dispose of the mask at the end of the day. Avoid using masks with valves.

take precautions against covid-19

things you should DO

wash your hand

Washing your hands is easy, and it’s one of the most effective ways to prevent the spread of germs. Experts suggest to scrub hands for at least 20 seconds.

always wear a mask

Masks should be worn by people 2 years of age and older. When you wear a mask, you protect others as well as yourself. Masks work best when everyone wears one.

stay home during fever

Most people with COVID-19 have mild illness and can recover at home without medical care. Do not leave your home, except to get medical care. Do not visit public areas.

things you should NOT DO

avoid close contact with animals

SARS-CoV-2 spreads primarily through human-to-human transmission, but there is evidence of transmission between humans and animals so it’s recommended to avoid contact with animals.

avoid close contact with peoples

Avoid physical contact like handshakes, hand holding or hugs. Always maintain a distance of about 6 feet. After coming home from outside, make sure to wash your hands.

avoid crowded area

Avoid visiting crowded and public places, it’s hard to maintain social distancing in places like this. Avoid touching surfaces such as table tops, chairs, door handles etc.
